How to Dress for Your Body Type: Complete Tops Guide

2026.03.01 · FITME Style Guide

How Necklines Affect Your Body Shape

The neckline is the first thing the eye goes to. Choosing the right one can visually change your shoulder width, neck length, and even the shape of your face.

V-neck: Guides the eye vertically, lengthening the neck and slimming the upper body. Especially effective for inverted triangle shapes. Crew neck / Round neck: Emphasizes the shoulders horizontally. Recommended for pear shapes — it broadens the upper body visually. Boat neck: Exposes the shoulder line, highlighting the collarbone. Avoid if you have a short neck. Turtleneck: Best for those with long necks and smaller faces.

💡 Face shape tip: Round faces benefit from V-necks; long faces suit crew or boat necks; square jaws are balanced by round or scoop necks.

Sleeve Types and Their Body-Shaping Effects

Puff sleeves: Visually widen the shoulders and upper body. Recommended for pear and rectangle shapes. Raglan sleeves: No seam at the shoulder creates a broader appearance. Inverted triangles should avoid this. Cap sleeves / Sleeveless: Best for those with slim arms; exposes the shoulder line. Drop shoulder long sleeve: The shoulder seam falls naturally for a relaxed, casual mood.

Top Length and Its Effects

Crop length (above the navel): Combined with high-rise bottoms, this maximizes leg length ratio. The best combo for shorter frames. Hip-grazing length: The most universally flattering length for all body types. Hip-covering length: Provides coverage for the lower body. Note: shorter frames should be careful as it can visually shorten the legs.

💡 The "half tuck" — tucking only the front of your top — gives you the best of both worlds: a defined waist without being fully cropped. Use it daily.
