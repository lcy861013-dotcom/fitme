Color Rules: How to Use Color to Flatter Your Body Shape

2026.03.15 · FITME Style Guide

Two Laws of Color in Fashion

1. Light expands, dark contracts: Light colors (white, ivory, beige, cream) make the areas they cover look wider. Dark colors (black, navy, charcoal) make those same areas appear slimmer.

2. Tone-on-tone creates height: Wearing the same color family from top to bottom allows the eye to travel smoothly from head to toe, creating the illusion of longer legs and a taller overall appearance.

💡 The simplest body-correction rule: dress areas you're less confident about in dark tones, and areas you're proud of in light or bright tones.

Inverted Triangle — Color Strategy

Goal: minimize the upper body, emphasize the lower body. Recommended: dark top + light or patterned bottom. Example: navy shirt + ivory wide-leg pants. Avoid: light top + dark bottom — this makes broad shoulders look even broader.

Pear Shape — Color Strategy

Goal: emphasize the upper body, minimize the lower. Recommended: light or patterned top + dark bottom. Example: white oversized top + black trousers. Avoid: dark top + light bottom.

Rectangle — Color Strategy

Goal: create the appearance of a waist. Recommended: color blocking — use different colors above and below the waistline. Example: white crop top + high-waist khaki pants. Adding a contrasting belt at the waist maximizes the effect.

5 Fail-Safe Color Combinations

1. Black + White: The timeless classic that works on every body type. 2. Navy + Cream: Elegant and soft. 3. Grey + Black: Monochrome done right. 4. Khaki + White: Natural and clean. 5. Brown + Beige: Warm earthy tones, always sophisticated.

💡 When wearing a pattern, keep it to one area: if your top has a print, keep the bottom solid. If the bottom is patterned, the top should be plain. This is the no-fail formula.
