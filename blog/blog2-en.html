Best Shoe Brands for Wide Feet

2026.02.01 · FITME Style Guide

What Is Foot Width? How to Measure Yours

Foot width refers to the widest part of your foot — the area connecting the joint of your big toe to the joint of your little toe. Even at the same shoe length, a wider foot can feel painfully tight or uncomfortably loose depending on the brand's last (the mold used to make the shoe).

💡 How to measure: Stand on a piece of paper and measure the widest part of your foot with a ruler. Average for adult men is approximately 3.8–4.0 inches (97–100mm); for women, about 3.5–3.6 inches (88–92mm). If your width is above average, use the guide below.

Top 5 Shoe Brands for Wide Feet

🥇 New Balance ↗: The top choice for wide-footed people. New Balance explicitly offers 2E (D) and 4E (2E) wide sizes. The 990, 993, and 1906R models are especially roomy in the forefoot.

🥈 Nike Dunk / Air Force 1 ↗: The boxy toe box construction gives a comfortable fit for moderately wide feet. Note: Air Max models tend to run narrower, so be cautious.

🥉 ASICS Gel Series ↗: Among running shoes, ASICS consistently offers the most generous width. The Gel-Kayano and Gel-Nimbus are especially recommended.

Salomon XT-6 ↗: Originally a trail running shoe, the XT-6's wide toe box is excellent for wider feet — and its chunky silhouette happens to be very on-trend for streetwear.

Adidas Samba / Gazelle ↗: These run slim by design. If you have wide feet, size up by half a size.

How to Buy Shoes Online Without the Guesswork

When reading reviews, look for phrases like "runs narrow," "tight in the toe box," or "not for wide feet." If you see 2+ mentions, go half a size up. If you see 3+, go a full size up.

💡 Your feet swell throughout the day and are largest in the afternoon. Try to shop for shoes in the afternoon, and always check the return policy before buying online.
