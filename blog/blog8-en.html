Waist-to-Hip Ratio: The Science Behind Your Style

2026.03.05 · FITME Style Guide

What Is WHR?

WHR (Waist-to-Hip Ratio) is your waist circumference divided by your hip circumference. For example: waist 27 inches, hips 38 inches → WHR = 27 ÷ 38 ≈ 0.71. This single number has a decisive effect on how clothes look and fit on your body.

💡 WHO health guideline: WHR below 0.85 for women and below 0.90 for men is considered healthy. From a fashion perspective, the lower this number, the more defined your waist line appears.

Style Strategies by WHR Range

WHR 0.65–0.75 (Hourglass): Your waist line is very defined. Fitted items, belts, and tuck-in styles maximize this natural advantage. Oversized items may hide your best feature — use them with intention.

WHR 0.75–0.85 (Balanced): The most versatile range. Both waist-defining and straight silhouettes work well. You can work with or without waist emphasis.

WHR 0.85+ (Rectangle / Apple): Less contrast between waist and hips. A-line skirts, high-waist items, and peplum details are effective at creating a waist illusion.

How to Calculate Your WHR

Step 1: Measure your waist at the narrowest point (usually just above the navel).
Step 2: Measure your hips at the widest point.
Step 3: Divide waist ÷ hips = your WHR.

Exercises to Improve Your WHR

To lower your WHR, you either reduce the waist (cardio + core exercises) or develop the hips (glute training). Squats, hip thrusts, and cable side bends are the classic WHR-optimization movements.
