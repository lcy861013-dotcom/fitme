How to Dress for Every Body Type: Complete Guide

2026.04.01 · FITME Style Guide

The Real Goal of Body-Type Styling

Dressing for your body type isn't about hiding flaws. It's about maximizing strengths and directing attention where you want it. Every body type has unique leverage points — the goal is knowing yours and using them.

Inverted Triangle (Broad Shoulders + Narrow Waist/Hips)

Your strength: Wide shoulders create a natural power silhouette.
Outfit formula: Keep tops simple (solid colors, V-necks) and add volume below (wide-leg pants, A-line skirts). Light-colored bottoms add hip volume to balance the proportions.
Avoid: Shoulder pads, puff sleeves, horizontal stripes on top.

Hourglass (Defined Waist + Balanced Shoulders and Hips)

Your strength: The waist line is your greatest asset.
Outfit formula: Tuck in your top, wear belts, and embrace wrap styles. Fitted clothing works in your favor across the board.
Avoid: Going fully oversized. It hides your best feature entirely.

Rectangle (Shoulders ≈ Waist ≈ Hips)

Your strength: The most versatile body type — you can wear almost anything.
Outfit formula: Create a waist with belts, high-rise items, and wrap styles. Layering adds depth and visual interest.
Try: Peplum tops, cropped blazers + high-waist pants.

Pear (Hips Wider Than Shoulders)

Your strength: A feminine hip line is your natural advantage.
Outfit formula: Add volume on top (wide collars, bright colors, structured shoulders) and keep the bottom simple and dark.
Avoid: Hip pockets, waist ruffles, wide belts — all of these draw attention to and widen the hip area.
