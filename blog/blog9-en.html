SHOULDER FIT

How Shoulder Width Changes Your Entire Outfit

2026.03.10 · FITME Style Guide

The Shoulder Is the Foundation of Every Top

When trying on a top, the first thing to check is the shoulder seam. If the seam falls more than half an inch past the edge of your actual shoulder, no fabric or tailoring in the world can make it look right. The shoulder is the anchor point for the entire garment.

💡 Fit check: the shoulder seam should land exactly at the outer edge of your shoulder bone (the acromion). If it falls outside, the top is too big. If it pulls inward, it's too small.

Best Top Strategies by Shoulder Width

Wide shoulders (18.5"+): The natural power silhouette of an inverted triangle. V-necks and raglan sleeves let the shoulder flow naturally. Avoid structured shoulder pads and puff sleeves — they'll push the width even further.

Average shoulders (16.5"–18"): The golden range. You can wear virtually any neckline and silhouette, from structured blazers to oversized drops.

Narrow shoulders (under 16"): Structured blazers with built-in shoulder padding, set-up jackets, and boat necks visually widen the frame. Avoid drop-shoulder styles — they push the visual shoulder point even lower and narrower.

Jacket Shoulders — The One Thing You Cannot Alter

In ready-to-wear clothing, the shoulder seam is the hardest alteration to make successfully. Sleeves, hem, and waist can be taken in or let out relatively easily. But raising or narrowing a shoulder seam is technically complex and often prohibitively expensive. Always fit the shoulder first when buying a jacket — everything else can be adjusted.
